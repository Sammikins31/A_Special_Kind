Portuguese Man O' War


Species:

Siphonophore (closely related to jellyfish but it isn't)
Lifespan: 1 year

Facts:

Looks cute but it is very dangerous! These are covered in venom which they use to paralyse and kill fish and other small creatures.

Their sting is extremely painful to humans but rarely deadly - If you get stung by the man o'war, it will feel like having hundreds of bee stings all at once! Ouch!

Sea Sheep or Leaf Sheep


(Constasiella Kuroshimae)

Species:

Sea Slugs
Lifespan: 2-3 years

Facts:

These sea slugs are extremely tiny- from 5 millimeters!
They only eat this particular algae called 'Avrainvillea' which you can see below.
When bigger animals are nearby, they use their toxic defence mecahnisms to fight them off!

These cute Sea Sheeps also glow!

Sea Bunny

Sea Bunny

(Jorunna Parva)

Species:

Marine Gastropoda
Lifespan: 3 months - 1 year

Facts:

Sea Bunnies are actually very small creatures of 1cm in length!

They may be cute but they are highly poisonous to any animal and human!
These Sea Bunnies eat toxic sponges, sea algae, sea grass and other small marine animals.

Flying Gurnard

Flying Gurnard

Species:

Dactylopterus Volitans
Lifespan: 1 year

Facts:

Looks similar to a flying fish but Flying Gurnards can't glide or fly in the air, but they dp glide on the surface of the water for a short period of time, with their colourful widespread pictorial fins.

They can get as big as 20 inches and weigh up to 4 pounds!

The word 'Gurnard' is french for grunt, because this fish makes growing sounds.
